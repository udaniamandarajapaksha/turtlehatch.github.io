Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

Located in the idyllic coastal town of Kosgoda, Sri Lanka, the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery stands as a beacon of hope for endangered sea turtles and a testament to the dedication of conservationists. Established with the aim of protecting and rehabilitating these magnificent creatures, the hatchery has become a vital institution in the battle against the decline of turtle populations. The hatchery serves as a sanctuary for several species of sea turtles, including the Olive Ridley, Loggerhead, Green, Hawksbill, and Leatherback turtles. These species face numerous threats in their natural habitats, such as habitat destruction, pollution, and poaching. At Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, their mission is to provide a safe space for these turtles to lay their eggs and ensure the survival of the next generation. The process at the hatchery begins with the collection of turtle eggs from vulnerable nesting sites along the coast. Dedicated conservationists carefully relocate these eggs to protected hatcheries within the facility. This safeguards them from predators and human interference, significantly increasing the chances of successful hatching. Once the eggs hatch, the baby turtles are carefully nurtured until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean. Education plays a crucial role at the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery. Visitors are provided with informative tours, where they learn about the life cycle of turtles, the threats they face, and the efforts being made to protect them. This awareness helps to foster a sense of responsibility and encourages individuals to take action to safeguard these remarkable creatures and their fragile ecosystems. Furthermore, the hatchery actively participates in conservation research and collaborates with local and international organizations to promote marine conservation efforts. They conduct studies on turtle behavior, nesting patterns, and the impact of environmental factors. These findings contribute to a better understanding of these species and inform conservation strategies on a broader scale. In addition to their conservation efforts, the hatchery also focuses on the rehabilitation of injured or sick turtles. With the help of veterinary experts, injured turtles are treated and nursed back to health. Once they have recovered, they are released back into the wild, giving them a second chance at life. The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery serves as a shining example of the positive impact that dedicated individuals can have on the preservation of endangered species. Through their tireless efforts, they not only protect turtles but also raise awareness about the importance of conservation among locals and visitors alike. By supporting the hatchery, people contribute to the ongoing conservation of these remarkable creatures and help ensure a sustainable future for marine life. Visiting the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery provides a unique opportunity to witness firsthand the remarkable work being done to protect and conserve sea turtles. It is a place where conservation meets education, inspiring visitors to become ambassadors for marine life conservation and fostering a deep appreciation for the beauty and fragility of our oceans.





Getting to know wild turtles in Kosgoda


Kosgoda is blessed with an abundant population of sea turtles, including the Olive Ridley, Green, Hawksbill, and Loggerhead turtles. These ancient reptiles have been swimming in the oceans for millions of years and play a vital role in maintaining the marine ecosystem.

Getting to Turtle Beach

Getting to Turtle Beach Kosgoda is a thrilling adventure. The beach is nestled along Sri Lanka's coastline, known for its mesmerizing beauty and thriving turtle population. With crystal-clear waters and golden sands, it's a paradise for nature lovers. Travelers can reach Turtle Beach by road, enjoying scenic views along the way. Once there, they can witness the incredible sight of turtles nesting or even observe baby turtles making their way to the ocean. Turtle Beach Kosgoda is a must-visit destination for an unforgettable wildlife experience.
