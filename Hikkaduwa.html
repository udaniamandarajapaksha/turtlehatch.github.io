Introduction to Turtle Beach in Hikkaduwa

Nestled along the pristine shores of Hikkaduwa in Sri Lanka, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery stands as a beacon of hope for endangered sea turtles. This sanctuary plays a vital role in protecting these magnificent creatures and raising awareness about their conservation. The hatchery focuses on five species of sea turtles: the Olive Ridley, Loggerhead, Green, Hawksbill, and Leatherback turtles. These species face numerous threats, including habitat loss, pollution, and illegal trade. The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery strives to counter these challenges and ensure the survival of these magnificent reptiles. At the hatchery, the process begins with the collection of turtle eggs from vulnerable nesting sites along the coast. Dedicated conservationists carefully relocate the eggs to protected areas within the hatchery, shielding them from potential dangers. This increases the likelihood of successful hatching and boosts the chances of survival for the baby turtles. Education is a key component of the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery's mission. Visitors are invited to take part in informative tours, where they learn about the life cycle of sea turtles, the threats they face, and the efforts being made to protect them. By raising awareness, the hatchery aims to instill a sense of responsibility and inspire individuals to contribute to turtle conservation. In addition to their conservation efforts, the hatchery plays an active role in rehabilitating injured or sick turtles. Through the expertise of veterinarians, these turtles receive necessary medical care and rehabilitation. Once they have recovered, they are released back into the ocean, giving them a chance to thrive in their natural habitat once again. The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery collaborates with local and international organizations, sharing knowledge and expertise to further enhance conservation efforts. They also conduct research on turtle behavior, nesting habits, and the impact of environmental factors. By expanding our understanding of these magnificent creatures, they contribute to the development of effective conservation strategies. Visiting the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a truly enriching experience. It offers a unique opportunity to witness the incredible work being done to protect sea turtles and their habitats. By supporting the hatchery, visitors contribute to the conservation of these endangered species and help secure a brighter future for marine life. The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery stands as a testament to the power of dedicated individuals in safeguarding endangered species. Through their unwavering commitment, they inspire us all to take action and preserve the fragile balance of our oceans. Together, we can protect these remarkable creatures and ensure their survival for generations to come. /p>



Getting to know wild turtles in Hikkaduwa

The Turtle Beach in Hikkaduwa is a popular place to find the local wild turtles and can be spotted by snorkelling over here all year round. These wild turtles roam freely and feed on the seaweed and lay their eggs for many years
